Our Impact

The impact of our device is significant on multiple levels.

Impact A

Qualitatively, it helps users express themselves and communicate, reducing the psychological and physical challenges they face. On a broader societal level, it promotes equality by providing an accessible and affordable solution for individuals with speech impairments.

Read more...

Impact B

The project also fosters interdisciplinary collaboration across fields such as computer science, biomedical engineering, mathematics, and economics, facilitating the exchange of knowledge and driving technological progress.

Read more...

Impact C

Quantitatively, we can measure the device’s impact by tracking the number of users, the production costs, and the accuracy of communication it facilitates. These metrics allow us to assess how effectively the device meets user needs and its potential for wider adoption. The project also brings together experts from at least five different fields, encouraging interdisciplinary work and accelerating the development of knowledge in those areas.

Read more...

Impact D

According to the Chinese Center for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) in 2020, the incidence of laryngeal diseases (including laryngeal cancer, inflammation of the larynx, etc.) was approximately 30 - 50 per 100,000 people. Given the population size in tier-1 and tier-2 cities, our product can bring a positive impact on an estimated 39,000-65,000 people who have laryngeal diseases. Given the working-age population percentage in China is around 68%, the GDP per capita is USD 12,700, and the estimated market penetration rate of our product of 10%, if patients can fully return to the labor market with our product’s assistance, we estimate our product can bring as much as 561.34 billion USD annually as our social impact.
